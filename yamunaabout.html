Yamuna 2013 Monsoon Flooding is the first Geotag-X pilot project, co-developed with Dr Sylvia Nagl of Yamuna's Daughters and Dr Rosita Haddad-Zubel. Yamuna's Daughters is a photography project for teenage girls living along New Delhi’s Yamuna River, which is the lifeline of the city’s water supply.


Contributors to this pilot project, including citizen photographers in New Delhi, are helping us test and improve Geotag-X modules about Yamuna flooding for use during future monsoon seasons. You are also welcome to contribute and to learn more about life on the Yamuna River. The aim is for crowd-sourced photographic image analysis to complement existing disaster response and recovery efforts, such as analysis of satellite images1 and field assessments.


More than 75,000 people live on the riverbanks of the Yamuna, a site of several disastrous floods in history2. The 2013 flood involved all of the rescue and relief measures available locally3 to evacuate residents and protect infrastructure45. We hope that crowd-sourced photo analysis could assist disaster responses in case of future flooding.
